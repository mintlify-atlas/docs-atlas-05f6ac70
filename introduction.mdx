## What makes Shield different

Shield combines client-side encryption with on-chain access control to deliver a security model you can verify independently — no need to trust Shield's servers.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="End-to-End Encrypted" icon="lock">
    Content is encrypted in your browser using AES-GCM 256 before it ever leaves your device. Shield never sees your plaintext.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Wallet-Native Auth" icon="wallet">
    Authenticate with your Ethereum wallet via Sign-In with Ethereum (SIWE). No email or password required.
  </Card>
  <Card title="On-Chain Access Control" icon="link-chain">
    Access policies — including recipient address, expiry, and attempt limits — are enforced by an immutable smart contract on Base.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Decentralized Storage" icon="database">
    Encrypted content is stored on IPFS via Pinata, providing censorship-resistant, content-addressed storage.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your wallet">
    Open [shieldhq.xyz](https://shieldhq.xyz) and connect your Ethereum wallet using RainbowKit or any WalletConnect-compatible wallet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload or write your content">
    Paste a message or upload a file. Shield encrypts it in your browser using AES-GCM 256 — your content never leaves your device unencrypted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set access conditions">
    Enter your recipient's wallet address, choose an expiry time, and optionally limit the number of access attempts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share the secure link">
    Shield creates an on-chain access policy and generates a unique link. Share it with your recipient — only they can decrypt the content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The secret decryption key is stored only in the URL fragment (`#key`) and is never sent to Shield's servers. Keep your link private.
</Note>
